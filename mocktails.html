Kombucha is thought to have originated in the Bohai Sea district of China. The first recipes are thought to date back to the start of the Qin Dynasty, around 221 B.C. Kombucha spread throughout Asia along the Silk Road, eventually reaching Russia and Europe. In Russia, it was known as "Kambucha" and in Germany as "Kombuchaschwamm".
